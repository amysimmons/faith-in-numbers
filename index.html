About the data

Religion in Australia

Hover over the bubbles to see how many people reported each religion - or no religion - in the 2011 census.

Catholicism had the largest population at 25%, followed by 22% of people who reported no religion.

No religion on the rise

In 1911 1 in 250 people reported no religion.

In 2011 that number had jumped to 1 in 5.

A dying age

This chart shows older people in Australia are considerably less likely than younger people to report no religion.

Only 10% of people over 65 reported no religion in 2011.

More civil weddings

Civil wedding ceremonies in Australia have outnumbered religious ones since 1999.

In 2011, 70% of all marriages were performed by a civil celebrant.

Ups and downs

While some areas of religion have declined, there has been an increase in affiliation to non-Christian religions.

Interesting finds

Pentecostalism, which is predominantly made up of young people, and Jedi, a following based on Star Wars, are among the religions that have incresed in recent years.

Hover over the bars to see the exact numbers.

Born overseas

Christians make up 56% of Australia's overseas born population. Non-Christians make up 19%.

Use the drop down menu to see what percentage of people in each religion is born in Australia or overseas.

Migrant influence

People who have recently arrived in Australia are less likeky to report Catholicism or Anglicanism than longer-standing arrivals.

They are more likely to report Hinduism, Islam and Buddhism.
